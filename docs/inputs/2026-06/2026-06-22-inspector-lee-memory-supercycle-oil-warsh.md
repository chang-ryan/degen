# Lee dump — memory super-cycle, oil deflation, Warsh sanguine

Three threads + a chart pack, all leaning *constructive* — a counterweight to the
cycle-top caution in [[ai-infra-cycle-top]]. The operating-model job: turn each into
a gauge (what would confirm/break it), not a verdict.

## 1. Buy the suppliers, not the buyers
Lee: the hyperscalers (much of Mag7) *not* working "makes a lot of sense — **buy what
the hyperscalers are buying (AI memory, optics/photonics) and not the hyperscalers
themselves.**" This *explains* the panel we already instrument (Mag7 breadth rolling
while SMH/memory rip) as a deliberate K-shaped trade, not a warning: the picks-and-
shovels capture the spend the buyers make.
- **Gauge:** a supplier-vs-buyer relative — memory/optics vs Mag7 (we have MAGS/SPY +
  the book; could add an explicit SMH-or-memory / MAGS ratio). Suppliers leading = thesis on.

## 2. Memory super-cycle — Jefferies expert call (the headline)
Jefferies' memory consultant: **3Q26 prices +40-50% QoQ, 4Q26 +30-40% QoQ** — *far*
above consensus (15-20% 3Q, <30% 4Q). **2027 +40-45% YoY.** **2028** could see an ASP
fall (15-20% supply growth from new capacity + slowing demand). **China not a threat
2026/7** (tech gap), though NAND could catch up by 2028. **LTAs now only with CSPs,
taking 50% of capacity (could go to 70%) → less supply for consumer electronics.**
- This is the supply-discipline story made concrete — and it *explains the Micron
  Crucial exit* (CSPs eating capacity, consumer squeezed out).
- **It dates the memory cycle top to ~2028, not now** — material for the "is it a top"
  question and the concentration/trim decision.
- **Gauge (the key new instrument):** actual DRAM/NAND **contract + spot prices** vs the
  +40-50% QoQ forecast (TrendForce/DRAMeXchange), and the memory names' **guidance** on
  the Jun-24 MU print onward. Confirm = prices print >consensus; break = China/supply
  surprise or CSP LTAs soften. The **2028 ASP-fall + China-NAND-catch-up** is the
  memory-specific cycle-top marker to pre-set.

## 3. Oil — crude materially lower over 6 months
Lee: "all we really care about is the flow of ships." **JPM desk: tankers ~2x pre-war.**
The Mideast risk premium should be *structurally* lower (adaptation — pipelines, new
ports, less SoH reliance, defensive drones). Crude "barely moved over four years"
through Ukraine / Russia-supply / the SoH closure — supply shocks didn't durably move
it; China EVs curtail demand. Revives the oil-deflation thesis (we banked USO) and is a
**disinflation tailwind into 2027**.
- **Gauge:** tanker/ship flow (JPM desk, ship-tracking), crude **term structure**
  (contango = oversupply), Brent/WTI vs the pre-conflict ~$55-60 floor. Supports a small
  defined-risk USO-put flyer; the macro read is disinflation.

## 4. Warsh / Fed — a hike doesn't scare him
Inflation "became a problem the day the first bomb dropped" → as oil normalizes, the
opposite (disinflation) should follow. FOMC projects **2.3% PCE next year**. A hike is
OK *if* it's a **hike-and-hold**, not a surprise-runaway-inflation hike — the factors
driving this economy won't change with a hike, and Warsh's comments suggest he gets
that. Labor improving but not driving wages; shelter/rent deflating. → softens our
"Warsh = vol catalyst, hold dry powder for the hedge" stance; Lee isn't rattled.
- **Gauge:** PCE trajectory vs the 2.3% proj, the SPX/Gold ratio (chart below), and *why*
  any hike happens (commodity-driven = transitory-ish; wage-driven = the scary kind).
