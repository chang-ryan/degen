## Thread 1 — MSTR / STRC collapse: the crypto-credit tail under the miners
Full writeup: [[mstr-strc-contagion]]. **The miners basket isn't just "AI compute + BTC beta" — it's also short the MSTR/crypto-credit tail.**

**State (6/18):** BTC **$64,553**, now **below MSTR's ~$66,385 cost basis**. MSTR $112.53, −43% off high, near lows. **STRC at $88.59** — a security *designed* to hold $100 par via monthly dividend resets, failing the peg even after the rate was cranked to **11.50%** (12.98% effective). ~$10.49B STRC notional; combined preferred dividends ≈ $1.5–2B/yr cash the software business can't cover.

**Mechanism (not a literal margin call):** MSTR isn't margin-lent vs its BTC, and maturities are 2028–30 — hard 2026 default is a tail. The real risk is a **reverse flywheel**: BTC down → mNAV compresses → equity-issuance window (which funds the preferred coupons) closes → forced choice between **selling BTC / hiking the STRC rate / suspending dividends** → each signals distress → spiral.

**Contagion to us:** MSTR holds ~3.4% of all BTC → forced selling overshoots BTC toward $45–50k → **guts the mining-economics floor** under the basket. AI-compute optionality doesn't save it in a crypto de-gross. **GLXY weakest** (crypto financial, not just a miner); MARA second (pure beta, near-dated Aug calls).

**The payoff — instrument it:** STRC's discount to par is a free, public, daily crypto-credit gauge that *leads* the miners.
- **STRC < ~$90 & falling** (now $88.59) → stress building → de-risk miners.
- **STRC < ~$80** → peg openly failing → cut hard.
- Corroborate: MSTR mNAV → 1, BTC < $60k.

## Thread 4 — "they make memory" fact-checks (CRSR, Kingston)
The supercycle pays **die-makers** (own the fab, own the scarcity: MU, SK Hynix, Samsung; storage WDC/SNDK/STX), not **assemblers** (buy dies, solder modules: CRSR, Kingston).
- **CRSR $8.92, flat** while MU rips — Corsair is gaming hardware that *buys* DRAM; rising DRAM = **higher COGS / margin squeeze**, a headwind. It's a Have-Not (consumer-discretionary PC peripherals), not a memory play. CRSR pops on a gaming-hardware catalyst, not the memory tape.
- **Kingston** — largest DRAM-module maker, absolutely cooking this cycle, but **private** (no ticker/bonds). Bigger than Corsair so it actually captures spread, but the only way to own it is the die-makers it buys from. **Test for any "memory" name: do they own a wafer fab, or buy dies?**
